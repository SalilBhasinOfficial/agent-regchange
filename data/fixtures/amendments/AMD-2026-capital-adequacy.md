# RBI/2026-27/12 — Amendment to the Master Direction on Prudential Norms on Capital Adequacy, 2025

*Notification dated April 11, 2026. Effective date: October 1, 2026.*

## Preamble
In exercise of powers conferred under sections 21 and 35A of the Banking
Regulation Act, 1949, the Reserve Bank of India hereby amends the Reserve
Bank of India (Commercial Banks — Prudential Norms on Capital Adequacy)
Directions, 2025 ("the Principal Directions"), with effect from October 1,
2026, as set out below.

## Amendment 1 — Insertion of Climate Risk Capital Buffer

In Chapter III, Part A.3 of the Principal Directions, after sub-paragraph (5)
of paragraph 11, the following new sub-paragraph (5A) shall be inserted:

> "(5A) **Climate Risk Capital Buffer (CRCB).** Every bank shall maintain
> an additional Climate Risk Capital Buffer of 0.25 per cent of RWAs in the
> form of CET1 capital, on an ongoing basis. The CRCB shall be phased in at
> 0.10 per cent of RWAs from October 1, 2026 and the full 0.25 per cent from
> April 1, 2027. The buffer shall be over and above the Capital Conservation
> Buffer."

## Amendment 2 — Quarterly ICAAP Refresh

In Chapter II, paragraph 7 of the Principal Directions, the words "at least
once a year" shall be substituted by "at least once every calendar quarter
and, additionally, immediately upon any material change in the bank's risk
profile."

## Amendment 3 — Board reporting on climate-related concentration

In Chapter II, Part A, paragraph 6 of the Principal Directions, after
sub-paragraph (iii), the following new sub-paragraph (iv) shall be inserted:

> "(iv) A bank shall have a Board-approved policy for assessment, monitoring
> and reporting of climate-related credit concentration in its loan book.
> The Risk Management Committee of the Board shall review such concentration
> at every meeting and the Board shall be apprised at least once every
> quarter."

## Amendment 4 — Pillar 3 disclosure for the CRCB

In Chapter II, Part A, paragraph 6, sub-paragraph (iii) of the Principal
Directions, the existing disclosure policy shall be deemed to include
disclosure of the Climate Risk Capital Buffer maintained by the bank, with
quantitative breakdown by exposure class, as part of the bank's annual
Pillar 3 disclosures effective from the financial year 2027–28.
