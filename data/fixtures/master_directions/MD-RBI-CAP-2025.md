## Chapter I — Preliminary

### A. Short title and commencement
1. These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks —
   Prudential Norms on Capital Adequacy) Directions, 2025.
2. These Directions shall come into effect immediately upon issuance.

### B. Applicability
3. These Directions shall be applicable to Commercial Banks (banking companies as
   defined in section 5(c) of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949), excluding RRBs,
   LABs, Payments Banks and Small Finance Banks.

## Chapter II — Board approved policies and scope of application

### A. Instructions regarding Board approved policies
6. A bank shall have a Board approved policy on the following matters pertaining to
   capital adequacy:
   (i)  The structure, design and contents of a bank's Internal Capital Adequacy
        Assessment Process (ICAAP), including stress testing assumptions.
   (ii) A bank shall have an explicit Board-approved capital plan which spells out
        the bank's approach to maintaining adequate capital over the planning
        horizon.
   (iii) A bank shall have a formal disclosure policy approved by the Board of
         Directors covering Pillar 3 disclosures.

7. A bank's Board of Directors shall assess and document, at least once a year,
   whether the policies, procedures and limits remain appropriate.

## Chapter III — Regulatory Capital

### A.3 Limits and minima
11. The limits and minimum capital requirements are as under:
    (1) A bank shall maintain a Minimum Total Capital (MTC) of 9 per cent of the
        RWAs on an ongoing basis.
    (2) CET1 capital shall be at least 5.5 per cent of the RWAs on an ongoing basis.
    (3) Tier 1 capital shall be at least 7 per cent of the RWAs on an ongoing basis.
    (4) Total capital (Tier 1 + Tier 2) shall be at least 9 per cent of the RWAs on
        an ongoing basis.
    (5) In addition to the minimum CET1 capital of 5.5 per cent of the RWAs, a bank
        shall maintain a Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB) of 2.5 per cent of RWAs.

### B. Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital
12. CET1 capital shall comprise the following:
    (i)    Common shares (paid-up equity capital) issued by the bank.
    (ii)   Stock surplus (share premium) resulting from the issue of common shares.
    (iii)  Statutory reserves.
    (iv)   Capital reserves representing surplus arising out of sale proceeds of
           assets.
    (v)    AFS — Reserve (per the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks —
           Classification, Valuation and Operation of Investment Portfolio of
           Commercial Banks) Directions). Any negative balance in the AFS — Reserve
           shall be deducted from CET1 capital.
    (vi)   Revaluation Reserves arising out of change in the carrying amount of a
           bank's property (subject to conditions a–f below).
    (xi)   While calculating capital adequacy at the consolidated level, common
           shares issued by consolidated banking subsidiaries to third parties
           (minority interest) may be included subject to limits.
    (xii)  Less: Regulatory adjustments / deductions applied in the calculation of
           CET1 capital.

### A.2 Elements of regulatory capital
10. Total regulatory capital shall consist of the sum of the following categories:
    (i)  Tier 1 Capital (going-concern capital):
         (a) Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Capital;
         (b) Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Capital;
    (ii) Tier 2 Capital (gone-concern capital).
