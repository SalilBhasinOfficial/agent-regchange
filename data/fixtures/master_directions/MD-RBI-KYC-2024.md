## Chapter I — Preliminary

### 1. Applicability
1. These Directions apply to all Scheduled Commercial Banks (excluding RRBs),
   Cooperative Banks, NBFCs, and Payment System Operators regulated by the
   Reserve Bank.

### 2. Definitions
2. "Customer Due Diligence" (CDD) means identification and verification of a
   customer at the time of commencement of an account-based relationship and
   on a periodic basis thereafter.

## Chapter II — Customer Due Diligence

### 3. CDD at on-boarding
3. A bank shall undertake CDD at the time of commencement of an account-based
   relationship, by:
   (i)  obtaining an Officially Valid Document (OVD) for proof of identity and
        address;
   (ii) verifying the OVD against the issuing authority's database where
        feasible;
   (iii) capturing a recent photograph of the customer.

### 4. Periodic updation
4. A bank shall carry out periodic updation of KYC records as follows:
   (i)   for high-risk customers — once every two years;
   (ii)  for medium-risk customers — once every eight years;
   (iii) for low-risk customers — once every ten years.

### 5. Beneficial ownership
5. A bank shall identify the beneficial owner of any non-individual customer
   and verify the identity of such beneficial owner.

## Chapter III — Record-keeping

### 7. Retention
7. A bank shall maintain records of all transactions, including those
   referenced in section 12 of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002,
   for a period of at least five years from the date of the transaction.
